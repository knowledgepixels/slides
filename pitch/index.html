Problem

  • Scientific knowledge and discourse are scattered, unstructured, and not interoperable
  • Existing approaches for organizing scientific knowledge (ResearchGate, ChatGPT, etc.) are shallow and imprecise
  • Science is slowed down by this lack of efficient communication

Solution:

Quick and Rich Knowledge Updates

  • Precise: Machine-interpretable due to logic-based representation
  • Fast: Created quickly due to minimal size, and disseminated quickly due to dedicated ecosystem
  • Trustworthy: Trust through social network and through rich statement-level provenance
  • Universal: Can capture findings, studies, researchers, arguments, opinions, datasets, etc. of any domain

Technology: Nanopublications

Universal approach for all kinds of scholarly knowledge, based on small and provenance-aware data snippets:

## Product: Nanodash - Platform to publish and view nanopublications, and to interact with scientific communities - Nanopublication feeds (like Twitter, but machine-interpretable) - Community pages (like Facebook or LinkedIn, but for research communities) - Domain-level knowledge base (like Wikipedia, but machine-interpretable and coming directly from the researchers)
## Market Validation University Spending Globally: - $1 billion/year APCs (Article Processing Charges) - &gt; 500k Open Access articles/year - avg. $1600/article - $6 billion/year on journal subscriptions - &gt; 2 million articles/year - $50 billion/year on technology
## Business Model (Short-Term) - **Partners:** - Funds from research projects and innovation grants - _Value:_ Help research communities prepare their future communication technology - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - Free use of Nanodash - _Value:_ Fast, precise, and trustworthy knowledge updates; visibility and recognition in community
## Business Model (Medium-Term): ### Multi-Sided Platform - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - Freemium model - `+` Value: credit for community-driven datasets; insight from precise aggregations and analyses - **Publishers / Journals:** - Subscription model - Value: Close connection to research communities; getting good reviewers/papers, visibility, reputation, FAIR
## Long-Term Value: - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - Time saved for getting/staying up-to-date in scientific field - AI-powered precise and trustworthy assistance with hypotheses, studies, interpretations, etc. - **Publishers / Journals:** - Role as guardians of scientific communities and data - Economic opportunities in new ecosystem/market - **Everybody:** - AI-powered formal reasoning on humanity's knowledge
## Plans and Pricing - **Nanodash for researchers (with group discounts):** - Free - Standard ($200/y/user) - Pro ($400/y/user) - **Nanodash for journals (with discounts for large ones):** - Standard ($12/y/article; <1% of avg. APC) - Pro ($18/y/article)
