Problem

  • Scientific knowledge and discourse are scattered, unstructured, and not interoperable
  • Existing approaches for organizing scientific knowledge (ResearchGate, ChatGPT, etc.) are shallow and imprecise
  • Science is slowed down by this lack of efficient communication

Solution:

Quick and Rich Knowledge Updates

  • Precise: Machine-interpretable due to logic-based representation
  • Fast: Created quickly due to minimal size, and disseminated quickly due to dedicated ecosystem
  • Trustworthy: Trust through social network and through rich and detailed provenance of every single statement
  • Universal: Can capture findings, studies, researchers, arguments, opinions, datasets, etc. of any domain

Technology: Nanopublications

Universal approach for all kinds of scholarly knowledge, based on small and provenance-aware data snippets:

Ecosystem

We are creating the knowledge space, an ecosystem to communicate science in a fast, precise, and trustworthy manner in small machine-understandable pieces (nanopublications).

## Product: Nanodash - Platform to publish and view nanopublications, and to interact with scientific communities - Nanopublication feeds (like Twitter, but machine-interpretable) - Community pages (like Facebook or LinkedIn, but for research communities) - Domain-level knowledge base (like Wikipedia, but machine-interpretable and coming directly from the researchers)
## Market Validation Article Processing Charges (APCs): - ~ 5 million papers published each year - &gt; 500k papers (rising trend) follow OA with APC model - ~ 1 billion USD of total yearly APCs (avg. $1600/article) University Technology Expenses: - Universities globally spend $50 billion/year on technology
## Business Model (Short-Term): ### Partnerships - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - Free - Value: Fast, precise, and trustworthy knowledge updates; visibility and recognition in community - **Research Projects / Innovation Funds:** - Value: Preparation of future research communication technology to help research communities
## Business Model (Medium-Term): ### Multi-Sided Platform - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - Freemium model - `+` Value: credit for community-driven datasets and vocabs; insight from precise aggregations and analyses - **Publishers / Journals:** - Subscription model - Value: Close connection to research communities; getting good reviewers/papers, visibility, reputation, FAIR
## Business Model (Long-Term): - **Researchers / Communities / Projects:** - `+` Value: time saved for getting and staying up-to-date in scientific field; AI-powered precise and trustworthy assistance with hypotheses, studies, interpretation, etc. - **Publishers / Journals:** - Value: Strong role as guardians of scientific communities and data, and related economic opportunities - **Funders / Institutions / Media / Public / Everybody:** - AI-powered formal reasoning on humanity's knowledge
